1        Overview

This system allows staff, students and guests to register for conferences online.

The system is very customizable. You can choose which kind of payments to allow, including credit cards, account numbers (entered, but not processed online), and scholarship numbers as well as check mailing instructions. The system will allow conference administrators to create and manage their own conferences. They can copy other conferences as well. They will be able to add any questions they want. They will be able to download an MS Access database or an Excel spreadsheet to use for more customized conference management onsite.

On the Campus Staff Site, staff members will be able to register, view reports and update scholarship payments. Student leaders and other staff who are not in the campus ministry are able to do these same things from a public site if given the web address and the proper passwords. They can also create and administrate conferences.

 

1.1           Architectural Overview

The system is programmed in Java and JSP pages and is hosted and maintained by the Campus Ministry alt.60M team in Orlando, FL.

2.2.5    Edit Payment Options:

 

 Full Cost The CRS is designed to allow for a conference price that changes after a particular date. This dependant on the discounts.

 Commuter Price Enter the total conference cost for students who indicate that they are not staying at the hotel/conference facility. You will need to select yes in the pre-made custom question section for the question: Are you commuting. If they check yes when registering, it will charge them the commuter price. If there is no commuter price difference, put the full cost in this field also.

 Registration Deposit Enter the amount students must pay to be pre-registered for the conference. Students are warned that if this amount is not received by the deadline, they will not be pre-registered.

 Full Payment Discount If you wish to give your students a discount if they pay the full conference cost with a credit card during pre-registration, enter the amount of the discount here. Otherwise, enter 0.

 Early Registration Discount If you wish to give your students a discount if they pay before a certain date, enter the amount of the discount here. Otherwise, enter 0.

 Early Registration End Date Enter the date of the price change. It cannot be earlier than the Date Pre-Registration Opens or later than the Date Pre-Registration Closes. If you only have one price, then simply enter any date between registration open and close dates.

 Checks Payable To Enter to whom students should make their payment checks payable. For example, Campus Crusade for Christ or Dallas Winter Conference.

 Authnet Merchant username/account # and Password

 Accept Credit Cards Answer Yes if you wish to allow students to make their on-line pre-registration payment by credit card. This requires a merchant account with Authorize.net and set up your account according to the instructions listed under Setting Up Your Region in this guide. Answer No if you do not want to give students this payment option.

 Accept Checks Answer Yes if you wish to allow students to mail in checks.  Answer No if you do not want to give students this payment option. You may wish to go into the admin section and enter the postmark date when you receive the check.

 Accept Scholarships Answer Yes if you wish to allow students to make scholarship entries online. They will be prompted to fill in the amount and the staff persons name to contact. Then you or the staff person themselves will need to go in later and fill in the chart field combination.

 Custom payment add-ons  You can add as many additional payment options as you want. These will be added as custom questions of a special type. They will appear on the My Registration Info page of the registration site. If the registrant chooses yes to an add-on, the dollar amount of the add-on will be added to their conference cost. Examples include, Ski trip, purchase a book, a t-shirt, etc.

3.3           Ministry Guest Registration

The only reason a guest needs to register is if they are staying in the hotel. If they arent, then we dont need to collect money from them, and it isnt critical that they be in our registration system. It is assumed that guests who register do so because they need a room in the hotel. Therefore, the only pertinent questions are when would they arrive/leave and do they have a roommate preference.

You may wish to give ministry guests (non-staff, non-student attendees such as pastors, recruiters, etc.) special access to register through the CRS. Since staff members have access not only to registration, but also to view student and staff lists, you may not wish to give ministry

guests access to the staff section of the CRS. Ministry guests can register for your conference by visiting a link you give them that is listed on the home page of the administrator tool. Or soon they will be able to use a generic URL such as http://conference.campusCrusadeForChrist.com/guest which will list all the conferences that are open for registration.

 

Their registration pages will mirror the staff registration pages without asking for account numbers or regional status. Ministry guests registration records will be added to the Staff table and will show up in staff reports. They are distinguished by having a registration type of guest.

4.3           Viewing reports:

The reports menu can be accessed from the staff site conference page by any staff member (or from the staff public page with a staff password) for the conference.

 

Staff members can access the following reports to help prepare for the conference:

 Staff Housing Report This report lists all staff members (and ministry guests) who have indicated they will be staying at the hotel/conference facility. It also lists arrival/leave dates, roommate preferences, and additional rooms needed for children.

 Child Care Requests This report lists all children whose parents have requested child care at the conference along with their birthdates and arrival and leave dates.

 Facilities Request This report lists all requests for facilities that staff members have made for the conference.

 Ministry Booth/Breakout Session Requests This report lists all requests by non-regional staff and ministry guests for booths or ministry breakout sessions.

 Student Ride Offers If you are collecting ride information from your students, this report will list the students who have offered to give rides and their city of departure.

 T-Shirt Size Requests If you have asked students to indicate T-shirt size during registration, this report will list the number of requests for each size.

4.4.3   Deleting Student Registrations

From the Manage Registrants screen, edit the student, then click the Delete button at the end of the page of the student you wish to delete.  Deleting a student registration with corresponding payment records will eliminate from the CRS any record that you received a check or that a credit card transaction took place. It will not refund the students credit card payment or delete them from the transaction batches in Authorize.net.  Deleting a student registration does not delete the Student record from the system, only the records pertaining to this particular conference. The student will be able to find their record by their email/username in the future.

 

4.4.4   Managing Student Payments

From the Manage Registration Info page, click on a registrant, then click the words Manage Payments near the end of the personal info section.  This will take you to a payments page that will allow you to manually edit payment records. You will need to do this when checks arrive in your office to confirm that payment has been received. For checks, enter the amount of the check, and the check number under Comment (optional). The date will automatically be saved.  If the check was postmarked after the discount date, make a note in the comment field so when the student gets to the conference there will be an explanation.

 

You may also use this page to delete payment records and manually add new records. To delete a record, click the delete button below the payment record. To add a new record, go to the section at the bottom of the page called Add New Payment, fill out the information for the new payment, and click Save. Note: Changing payment records does not affect transactions in Authorize.net.

 

4.4.5   Managing Incomplete Registrations

When a student begins registration and exits after selecting a payment method, but before completing the payment process, they have an incomplete registration. This will also occur if Authorize.net returns an unexpected error. In this instance, they will have a student registration record, but no payment record, and therefore are not considered pre-registered. To view a list of these students, locate the link for: Incomplete Registrations on the Manage Registration Info page. This will load a page listing the incomplete student registrations. To email all these people, click the E-mail These Registrants link which will bring up a blank email in your default email program with these registrants email addresses in the to field. If you want to delete these registrants, you can click on each one to get to the screen where you can view, edit, or delete their registration record. They will still have their log in and contact info saved in the general student database, but they will have to register again and answer all the My Registration Info questions.

 

4.5           Managing Staff Registrations

You can view a complete list of staff members registered for a particular conference from the Regional Administrators Menu. Under the heading View Complete Staff Registration List. Note: This list includes ministry guests as well. You can delete a staff members registration by clicking the word Delete at the end of their record. You will have an opportunity to view their registration record to confirm. To delete the registration, click the button Delete This Registration. Note: Deleting a staff members registration record will delete registration information for their entire family unit.

5        Component: Downloading/Uploading Your Conference Data

Downloading Your Conference Data

You can download either a Microsoft Access or Excel version of the data. To download an Access database for use on-site at your conference:

-Log into the conference.  

-Go to the section "Download Reg. Info"

-Click on the template that you wish your data to be placed into. If you have a custom database, you can download using the blank database template which will be filled with all the newly generated tables, then copy these tables to your database where your reports and queries are stored.

-Wait for your database to be generated (up to a minute)

-Right click on the link supplied, to download your database.

 

Downloads and Uploads are secure (they use SSL). 

 

Uploading Your Conference Database

If you are a small conference, don't worry about uploading you data. This is mainly for big winter conferences. The main benefit for this function is to get finalized lists of who went to the conference for campus staff to access. This would be adding the walk-ins that registered and were entered at the conference and deleting the no-shows (removing their registration, but not their person record or log in account from the student database).

 

Before uploading the database, you should compact it. This will greatly reduce the size of the file and save you upload time. Heres how: open up the database and click:
Tools -> Database Utilities -> Compact and Repair Database

 

Assuming you have followed the guidelines listed below, return to the "Download Reg. Info"  section of the website.

Find the Access Database Upload section and browse to find your database file.

Click upload and be very patient, as it may take several minutes to first upload, and then process your database.

 

  • When you upload a conference, it removes no-show registrations from the national server (just their registration, so they no longer show up on your web reports), and adds walk-in attendee information
  • For uploading you need to have an email address for each person. (They will be skipped otherwise)
  • Custom answers or finance records will not be uploaded.
  • You may create new tables, queries, forms and macros in this database.
  • You may modify any tables in it with the following caveats:
    • In the Registrants table, if you modify any of the structure of the fields prior to the registrationID field, you will not be able to upload walk-in or no-show information after the conference.  You may add, modify, or remove any fields after the registrationID field.
    • The Registrants table lists registrants who registered online for your conference with personID numbers having values less than 5,000,000. You will notice that walk-ins will be assigned Student ID numbers higher than 5,000,000. If you modify this table in any way that changes this configuration, you will not be able to upload walk-in information after the conference.  This includes exporting these records to another table which you use for registration and changing the personID field from the type auto-number to type number. 

 

 

Creating a template for download use.

If you want us to post a copy of your database to be used as a template from the download page, follow the procedures below.

You may want to download a current template from the conference download page to modify to be used as your template.

You may:
Edit/Add Forms
Edit/Add Your own tables
Delete Auto-Generated tables - they will be re-created each time someone downloads using this template.

You may not:
Modify any of the Auto-Generated tables (see below for list) because any changes you make will be overwritten by our system.

 

When you submit this template to be added to our system (submit to the Campus Ministry Information Solutions Team at alt.60m@uscm.org), it is advisable to remove all non-relevant data, and compact the database.  (Delete Auto-Generated tables to save space since they will be recreated.)

Auto-Generated tables: ChildRegistration, Conference, CustomQuestions, GuestRegistration, Payments, Registrants, Schools, StaffRegistration, StudentRegistration

 

When creating your template, you may wish to add fields to the Registrants table such as walk-in? or name tag printed?.  When the template is used to create the downloaded database, the Registrants table WILL be deleted and recreated.  Here is how you tell our system to recreate your custom fields.  Before sending us your template, create your custom fields in the Registrants_Custom table.  Our system will append your custom fields to the Registrants table that it generates.
